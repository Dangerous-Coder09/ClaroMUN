About the Committee

Learn what delegates will discuss, research, and practice in WHO.

Northeast India occupies a position of immense strategic, economic, and cultural significance. Connected to the rest of the country through the narrow Siliguri Corridor, the region shares international borders with China, Myanmar, Bangladesh, Bhutan, and Nepal, making it vital to India's national security and its Act East Policy. Rich in natural resources, biodiversity, and cultural diversity, the Northeast serves as an important gateway for trade and connectivity with Southeast Asia. Despite its importance, the region has witnessed several internal conflicts driven by ethnic tensions, identity-based politics, demands for autonomy, competition over land and resources, and concerns regarding migration and representation. These conflicts have periodically led to violence, displacement, and instability, affecting both regional development and national security. Among these, the ongoing unrest in Manipur has drawn particular attention, highlighting the challenges of balancing diverse community interests while preserving social harmony.

The persistence of such conflicts underscores the need for inclusive governance, equitable development, and effective dialogue mechanisms to ensure lasting peace and strengthen national integration.
