Minutes To Midnight

Executive Committee of the National Security Council

Inside Kennedy's War Cabinet: Diplomacy, Brinkmanship, and Survival

About the Committee

Learn what delegates will discuss, research, and practice in ExCOMM.

Set against the most perilous moment of the Cold War, this committee places delegates inside President John F. Kennedy’s inner circle on 16th October 1962, the day the United States confirmed the presence of Soviet nuclear missiles in Cuba. As members of the War Cabinet, delegates are entrusted with the highest stakes of decision-making: balancing military aggression, diplomatic restraint, and global perception under extreme time pressure. With the world teetering on the brink of nuclear catastrophe, every directive carries consequences that could either preserve fragile peace or trigger irreversible escalation. From naval blockades and air strikes to back-channel negotiations and nuclear preparedness, delegates must navigate a volatile landscape of incomplete intelligence, political pressure, and strategic uncertainty. In this simulation, miscalculation is not just possible-it is probable. The line between deterrence and destruction is razor-thin, and a single decision could ignite a chain reaction leading to full-scale nuclear war. The survival of nations, and perhaps humanity itself, rests in the hands of the War Cabinet.
